## The Story

In 2017, I joined Yieldstreet as a software engineer when the team could fit in a single room. By 2025, when the company rebranded to Willow Wealth, we had built one of the leading platforms for private market investments, serving hundreds of thousands of investors.

This is the story of the product and platform decisions that got us there.

## Phase 1: Engineering the Foundation (2017–2018)

Joining as an engineer gave me something invaluable: deep understanding of the technical constraints and possibilities of our platform. I worked on:

- **Core investment flows**: Building the transactional backbone that handled investor onboarding, accreditation, and fund allocation
- **Compliance automation**: Financial services means regulatory overhead — we automated what we could to reduce friction for both operations and investors
- **Data pipelines**: Setting up the analytics infrastructure that would later inform every product decision

The engineering phase taught me that the best product decisions come from people who understand what the system can and cannot do.

## Phase 2: The Product Pivot (2018–2021)

I transitioned to product management because I saw the gap: we had strong engineering but needed someone who could translate investor needs into technical requirements while keeping the architecture scalable.

Key initiatives during this period:

- **Greenfield product launches**: Took multiple new investment verticals from concept to launch, each with unique regulatory, UX, and technical requirements
- **Platform unification**: As we added asset classes, I led the effort to create a unified investor experience across previously siloed products
- **Self-service tooling**: Built internal tools that let operations and compliance teams manage offerings without engineering tickets

### The Lesson: Eat Your Own Dogfood

The most impactful product improvement during this period came from spending a week shadowing our investor relations team. Their workarounds and manual processes revealed platform gaps that no amount of analytics would have surfaced.

## Phase 3: Scaling Product Leadership (2021–Present)

As Senior PM and then Director, the challenges shifted from building features to building systems:

- **Product-led growth**: Implemented experimentation infrastructure and self-serve flows that reduced time-to-first-investment
- **Platform thinking**: Shifted the team's mental model from "building features" to "building capabilities" — reusable components that could serve multiple products
- **Cross-functional alignment**: With four engineering squads, two design teams, and a growing marketing org, the coordination challenge became the product challenge

### The Rebrand

The transition from Yieldstreet to Willow Wealth wasn't just a name change — it was a product strategy shift. We expanded from alternative investments into broader private markets, which meant:

- Rethinking the information architecture for a wider product surface
- Redesigning investor onboarding for different risk profiles
- Building new compliance workflows for additional asset types

## Patterns for Platform Builders

1. **Start as an engineer if you can.** The technical empathy you build is irreplaceable. You'll know when a "simple feature request" is actually a platform rewrite.

2. **Invest in internal tools early.** Every hour spent building self-service tooling for operations saves ten hours of engineering time over the next year.

3. **Unify before you multiply.** Adding a new product to a fragmented platform creates exponential complexity. Unify the architecture first, then expansion becomes linear.

4. **Measure adoption, not just deployment.** A feature that ships but doesn't get used is worse than a feature that never ships — it adds maintenance burden with no value.
